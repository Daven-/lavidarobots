Vision and Strategy

Vision

  1. • Develop social-good robot technology that will bring long-term acclaim to the Phoenix Tech and Stem community

Strategy

  1. • Use global technical competitions to motivate Phoenix area experts and Stem students to collaborate with each other
  2. • All research and prototyping occurs with weekend extracurricular “volunteers”
  3. • Always keep a minimum percentage (at least one-third)of team slots open for diverse Stem talent regardless of age
  4. • IRS 501(c)(3) non-profit objective and stakeholders
  5. ∴ Scientific research, robot prototyping and competition participation for “social-good and public-benefit to the Phoenix community and Arizona”

Social Good Aspect

  1. • The team focuses on robotic creations and scientific research for global technical competitions
  2. ∴ Solutions for sporting, home/personal assistance for the young and old, disaster containment, wildlife conservation, or any high-impact social-good and public benefiting application
  3. • Technical and competition solutions that are completely open-source Hardware and Software during and after development
  4. ∴ Research ideas and breakthroughs are shared so that the global technical environment is further fueled
  5. • Social-Good: Any potential technical competition winnings donated to selected university/school or under-represented (women or minority) STEM-related Phoenix area non-profit entity
  6. • Above all else, ensure all contributors have FUN through personal satisfaction, learn immensely, and utilize entrepreneur/leadership/collaboration/innovation skills along the way
